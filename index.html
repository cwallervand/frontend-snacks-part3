Let me tell you, Office Space—very, very tremendous movie, folks. I’ve seen a lot of movies about business, about the workplace, but nothing quite like this. Peter Gibbons, great guy, so relatable, let me tell you. He works in one of these terrible corporate offices—total disaster!—with cubicles everywhere, bad coffee, and this unbelievable boss, Bill Lumbergh, who just wants those TPS reports. I know paperwork, nobody more than me, but these TPS reports are a total mess, believe me.

Peter gets hypnotized—great move, very smart—and he stops caring about the ridiculous nonsense at work. Suddenly, he’s not working weekends, not listening to his lousy boss. Lot of people would say, "Peter, you’re a genius." And I agree, I totally agree. He and his friends, great team—Michael Bolton (not the singer, okay?), Samir—they cook up a plan to stick it to the company, just like a lot of us always wanted to. It’s truly fantastic.

There’s even a guy named Milton, absolutely hilarious. He just wants his stapler, folks, just a simple stapler. But the big bosses, they don’t care, no respect for the little guy—which is exactly what’s wrong with so many companies in America, trust me.

At the end, a fire clears it all out—total clean slate. Very dramatic. The movie’s about standing up to terrible management, doing what you want, and living the American dream. And nobody loves the American dream more than me. Believe me, Office Space is a classic. Everybody loves it, everybody’s talking about it. Tremendous film.
